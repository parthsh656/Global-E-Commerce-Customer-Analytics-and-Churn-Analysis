# Global E-Commerce Customer Analytics & Churn Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes customer behavior, revenue distribution, and churn patterns for a global e-commerce business using SQL, Python, and Power BI. The objective was to transform raw customer data into actionable business insights that can support customer retention, loyalty program optimization, and revenue growth strategies.

The project follows an end-to-end analytics workflow, starting from data exploration and business analysis in SQL, progressing through exploratory data analysis (EDA) and customer segmentation in Python, and culminating in an interactive Power BI dashboard for stakeholder reporting.

---

## Business Problem

An e-commerce company wanted to understand:

* Which customer segments generate the most revenue
* What factors contribute to customer churn
* Which acquisition channels attract valuable customers
* How customer behavior varies across regions and loyalty tiers
* How retention strategies can improve customer lifetime value

## Key Insights

### Revenue Concentration

* Champions generated approximately 58% of total revenue.
* Champions and Loyal Customers together contributed nearly 87% of total revenue.

### Customer Behavior

* Total Orders and Total Spending showed a strong positive correlation (0.82).
* Customer Lifetime Days and Total Orders showed a positive correlation (0.67).

### Churn Analysis

* Days Since Last Purchase had a strong relationship with churn (0.68).
* Customers with longer inactivity periods were significantly more likely to churn.
* At-Risk and Lost Customers demonstrated the highest churn rates.

---

## Business Recommendations

* Implement VIP retention programs for Champions.
* Convert Potential Loyalists into Loyal Customers through targeted loyalty campaigns.
* Launch win-back campaigns for At-Risk customers.
* Monitor customer inactivity as an early churn warning indicator.
* Invest in acquisition channels that attract high-value customers.
